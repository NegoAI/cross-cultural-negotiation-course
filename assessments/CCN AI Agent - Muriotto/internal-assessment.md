## Dimension 1 — Domain Rigour: 93/100

**Confidence: Medium** — chunk substance is strong on a textual read, but final depth-of-content judgement is partly the instructor's; citation existence is High-confidence (verified).

**Evidence:**
- Every KB chunk follows a disciplined template: a bolded principle, an explanation, a labelled "Negotiation example", a "Caveat", a "Strategic link", and a "Citation". E.g. *"BATNA means Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement… It is not the desired outcome, the opening demand, or a threat; it is the realistic fallback"* (KB 1.2), with a cloud-provider worked example and a culturally-mediated caveat. This is substance, not framework-naming.
- **Both halves covered deeply.** Part 1 (Negotiation Methodology) carries preparation, interests vs positions, stakeholder mapping, post-negotiation review, BATNA/ZOPA/reservation value, leverage, objective criteria, integrative/distributive, logrolling, post-settlement settlement, anchoring, concession sequencing, trust-building, opening moves, coalitions/internal alignment, and negotiation biases. Part 2 treats all five frameworks substantively with negotiation implications and limitations: Hofstede (six dimensions + ecological-fallacy caveat), Trompenaars (universalism/particularism, specific/diffuse, neutral/affective, achievement/ascription, sequential/synchronic), Meyer's eight Culture Map scales, GLOBE (practices-vs-values, assertiveness, performance/humane), Hall (high/low context, monochronic/polychronic), plus an explicit "Framework tensions and overlaps" chunk.
- **Citations verified.** The reference list is extensive and overwhelmingly canonical (Fisher/Ury/Patton, Lewicki, Malhotra & Bazerman, Raiffa, Hofstede, Hall, Trompenaars, Meyer, House/GLOBE, Salacuse, Brett, Lax & Sebenius, Tversky & Kahneman). - Five real documented cases (DaimlerChrysler, Renault–Nissan, Danone–Wahaha, Walmart-Germany, US–Japan auto talks) each carry a caveat warning against single-cause cultural attribution.
