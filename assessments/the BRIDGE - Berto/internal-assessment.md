## Dimension 1 — Domain Rigour: **90/100**

**Confidence: Medium-High.** Citation verification and both-halves coverage are reliable textual checks; chunk-substance judgement is partly the instructor's read.

**Evidence:**

1. **Chunk substance is consistently strong.** Each KB chunk follows a principle → explanation → concrete worked example → cross-links → source-notes pattern. E.g. *"No-deal discipline and agreement viability"* (Part 1 §1.1): defines BATNA and reservation point, then *"A development agency may prefer a three-year international training program, but agreement may be viable only if cost, quality standards, language delivery, compliance duties, and reporting requirements fit as a package"* — substance, not naming.

2. **Both halves of the course covered substantively.** Negotiation methodology (Part 1, five sections): preparation architecture, BATNA/ZOPA/reservation points, leverage and power-behind-the-table, value creation/claiming/package design, logrolling and contingent agreements, communication-as-diagnosis, trust diagnosis (ability/benevolence/integrity), ethics/impasse, implementation, governance, multi-round learning. Cross-cultural frameworks (Part 2): Hofstede dimensions, Hall high/low context, Trompenaars dilemmas, Meyer, GLOBE/House practices-vs-values, Earley & Ang Cultural Intelligence (all four CQ facets given their own chunks). Both halves are deep, not gestural.

3. **Frameworks treated as hypotheses, not labels.** *"Cultural dimensions are useful when they organize better questions, not when they become labels"* (§2.1). The anti-essentialist stance (McSweeney, Osland & Bird) is woven throughout rather than tacked on.

4. **Citations: all verified, zero fabricated.** Canonical course texts (Fisher/Ury, Lewicki, Malhotra & Bazerman, Raiffa, Hofstede, Hall, Trompenaars, Meyer, GLOBE/House, Earley & Ang) verified on sight. Twelve non-canonical/oddly-specific citations web-searched (≥2 variations) — all real (see citation table). This is a notably well-sourced KB.

**Why not higher:** Source attributions are given as *"Source notes:"* without years or titles in most chunks (existence is verifiable from author names, but the in-chunk citation is loose). A handful of chunks pack many sources into one note, which the instructor should spot-check for accurate attribution (existence-only is verified here; claim-attribution is not).

**Instructor-judgement flag:** substantive accuracy of domain claims is not verified by this grader.

## Dimension 2 — Generalisation: **92/100**

**Confidence: Medium.** Axis-count and exceptions-as-sections are checkable; depth is partly judgement.

**Evidence:**

1. **Genuinely multi-axis, not single-test-case.** The KB is built axis-by-axis rather than around one country pair: communication context (§2.3), hierarchy/authority (§2.2, §2.4), trust pathways (§2.4), time orientation (§2.4), decision-making/consensus (§2.4), disagreement/face (§2.3), uncertainty/risk (§2.2). Each has its own chunks with substance — well beyond a one-line mention.

2. **Exceptions present as their own dedicated section.** Part 3 §3.3 *"Exceptions, organizational context, professional norms, and individual variation"* is a full section with five chunks covering: culture-as-one-layer, organizational culture/artifacts, tight/loose + professional + sector norms, headquarters/subsidiary tensions, **biculturalism and frame-switching** (own chunk), and regional-prototype caution. This is the hardest test for this dimension and it is met cleanly.

3. **Generational shift and expatriate/international-experience variation explicitly handled.** §2.1 *"Cultural change and sensemaking over time"* covers generational shift, globalization, migration, remote work. §3.3 *"Biculturalism, international experience, and frame-switching"* covers expatriate adaptation. Regional variation within a region treated under prototype caution (*"A 'Nordic,' 'Latin American,' 'East Asian,' or 'Middle Eastern' pattern may hide major differences in context"*).

4. **Country material is bounded and hypothesis-framed.** Part 3 §3.4 gives Japan, US–Japan/East Asia–North America, Latin America, dignity/face/honor, cross-border digital — each tagged as a *"testable hypothesis"* with non-cultural alternatives, not a deterministic profile.

**Why not higher (90–100 band justification met):** the exceptions-as-own-sections test — the hardest-test feature for this dimension — is unambiguously satisfied, which earns the 90+ band. Held just below the ceiling because the country library, while bounded, leans toward East Asia / Latin America exemplars; broader axis coverage (e.g. a second dignity-culture or a South Asia card) would round it out.
