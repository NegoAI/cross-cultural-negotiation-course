## Dimension 1 — Domain Rigour — **94 / 100**

**Confidence:** Medium — citation existence and structural depth are reliable textual checks (high); chunk-substance quality is partly a judgement read (medium).

**Evidence**
- **Both halves of the course are deep, not named.** Part 1 (Negotiation Methodology) carries BATNA/reservation/ZOPA (1.1), interests vs positions and value creation/claiming with the negotiator's dilemma (1.2), anchoring/concessions/leverage (1.3), objective criteria, multi-issue packages, Salacuse's ten-factor profile and Sebenius blind spots (1.4). Part 2 covers all five required frameworks substantively — Hofstede (with the ecological-fallacy chunk and an explicit note on the omitted Indulgence dimension), Meyer's full eight scales, Trompenaars' seven dimensions, Hall as a dedicated foundational section (2.4), GLOBE practices-vs-values + Assertiveness + Performance/Future/Humane Orientation, and a Framework Tensions section (2.5) that resolves three named conflicts.
- **Chunk discipline is consistent.** Nearly every chunk follows principle → explanation (with explicit *Negotiation relevance*) → *Example*, plus a *Strategic link* connecting mechanics to culture (e.g. 1.1 BATNA links to Meyer Communicating 2.2 and "explicit BATNA signalling can damage the relationship before bargaining begins").
- **Critical-distinction chunks show real understanding, not labelling.** The KB separates Leading from Deciding ("the error of treating 'who is respected in the room' as 'who can bind the organisation'"), Evaluating from Disagreeing, Communicating from Disagreeing, and UAI from tight/loose norms — exactly the conflations a weaker KB collapses.
- **Every citation verified; full bibliography present.** All canonical course texts plus a long tail of specific sources. Web-checked the riskiest non-canonical items — Osman-Gani & Tan (2002, *Thunderbird IBR* 44(6):819–839, DOI confirmed), Krug & Rothlin (2010, RSM Case 310-105-1), Cordeiro (2018/19, Palgrave Handbook ch.16), Gerlach & Eriksson (2021, *Frontiers in Psychology* 12:662604), Bu (2011, *EJLR* 13(3–4)), Hollmann et al. (2010), Satake (2000, ANU Asia Pacific) — all exist as attributed. **Zero fabricated citations.**

**Why not higher:** the only deductions are judgement-side, not defect-side — a couple of regional profiles (Mexico, Southern Europe) carry lighter source backing than the East Asia/Gulf profiles, and the Hollmann et al. journal name in the bibliography (Revista de Administração Contemporânea) is a mis-attribution of venue (the paper ran in REA/UFSM) — an attribution detail, not an existence problem, flagged for the instructor.

**Why not higher:** within-country variation is acknowledged as a coverage gap rather than fully built out for every region — appropriate honesty, but it caps the very top band marginally.

**Hardest-test feature earning 90+:** exception layer as its own multi-subsection part, with biculturalism, acculturation *strategies* (not just "people vary"), and CQ each treated as analytic lenses tied to observable signals.
